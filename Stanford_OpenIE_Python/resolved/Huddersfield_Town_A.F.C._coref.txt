Jonas Bybjerg Lössl (born 1 February 1989) is a Danish professional footballer who plays as a goalkeeper for club Huddersfield Town.

A grandparent of Jonas Lössl is German. For this reason, A grandparent of Jonas Lössl surname is written with "ö" instead of "ø" which is otherwise the normal spelling of this letter in the Danish language.

A product of the FC Midtjylland football academy, Lössl made Lössl Danish Superliga debut with FC Midtjylland in March 2010. Lössl played a total number of 137 matches for the club.

On 16 June 2016, it was reported that Lössl was making a move to German club Mainz 05. the club cited that they intended Lössl to be the instant replacement for the club's previous keeper, Loris Karius, who himself had just transferred to Liverpool.

After one season at German club Mainz 05, Lössl signed for Huddersfield Town on a season-loan on 30 June 2017. On 12 August 2017, Lössl made Lössl debut for Huddersfield in a 3–0 win against Crystal Palace.
